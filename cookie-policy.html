Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2024

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our website. They serve multiple purposes including remembering your preferences, tracking your usage patterns, and enhancing your browsing experience. Cookies may be persistent (stored until deleted) or temporary (session cookies that expire when you close your browser).

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential/Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as:

  • User authentication and session management
  • Security features and fraud detection
  • Storing your login information
  • Maintaining your shopping cart
  • Enabling form submissions

Status: Cannot be disabled; required for site functionality

Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with our website:

  • Tracking page views and navigation patterns
  • Identifying popular content and features
  • Measuring website performance
  • Identifying technical issues
  • Analyzing user demographics and behavior

Status: Optional; can be disabled through cookie settings

Preference Cookies

These cookies remember your choices to personalize your experience:

  • Language preferences
  • Theme selection (light/dark mode)
  • Accessibility preferences
  • Volume settings for media
  • Previously visited pages

Status: Optional; can be disabled

Marketing Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver targeted advertising and marketing messages:

  • Tracking your interests across websites
  • Displaying relevant advertisements
  • Measuring marketing campaign effectiveness
  • Creating targeted audiences
  • Retargeting ads based on browsing history

Status: Optional; requires explicit consent

Third-Party Cookies

Cookies set by third parties such as:

  • Analytics providers (Google Analytics)
  • Payment processors
  • Social media platforms
  • Advertising networks
  • Customer support tools

Status: Subject to third-party privacy policies

3. Similar Tracking Technologies

Beyond cookies, we may use other tracking technologies:

  • Web Beacons/Pixels: Transparent images used to track page views and user interactions
  • Local Storage: Browser storage similar to cookies for storing user preferences
  • Device Identifiers: Unique identifiers for tracking mobile app users
  • Server Logs: Recording IP addresses and user activity

4. How to Control Cookies

Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies and other site data

Cookie Consent Banner

Upon visiting our website, you may see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Accept only essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences
  • Manage preferences at any time

5. Opt-Out Options

To opt out of specific tracking:

  • Google Analytics: Install Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Advertising: Visit Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) or Network Advertising Initiative (NAI)
  • Email Marketing: Click "Unsubscribe" in marketing emails
  • Do Not Track: Enable "Do Not Track" in your browser settings

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Disabling cookies may affect your experience:

  • You may not be able to log in to your account
  • Your preferences may not be saved
  • Site functionality may be impaired
  • You may need to re-enter information repeatedly
  • Some features may not work properly

7. Data Retention

We retain cookie data in accordance with our Privacy Policy. Cookie lifespan varies:

  • Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies are stored until their expiration date
  • You can manually delete cookies from your browser
  • We regularly review and clean up expired cookie data

8. Compliance with Privacy Laws

We comply with global privacy regulations:

  • GDPR (Europe): Obtaining explicit consent for non-essential cookies
  • CCPA (California): Providing cookie disclosure and opt-out rights
  • India: Following IT Act guidelines on user data and tracking
  • ePrivacy Directive: Complying with European cookie regulations

9. Third-Party Links

Links to external websites may use their own cookies. We are not responsible for third-party cookie practices. Please review their privacy and cookie policies for their specific practices.

10. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy periodically. Changes will be reflected on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. Your continued use of our website constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact Us: If you have questions about our cookie practices, contact us at privacy@cyberztalk.com
