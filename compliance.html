Compliance & Legal Framework

Our commitment to legal compliance and cybersecurity standards

1. Indian Legal Framework

Information Technology Act, 2000

The primary legislation governing cyber crimes and electronic commerce in India. Cyberztalk operates in compliance with key sections:

Section 43 - Unauthorized Access:

Prohibits unauthorized access to computer systems. Users reporting such incidents can seek remedies through Cyberztalk's platform.

Section 66 - Computer Crimes:

Addresses unauthorized access to protected systems, with punishment up to 3 years imprisonment and ₹5,00,000 fine. We facilitate reporting of such crimes.

Section 67 - Obscene Materials:

Prohibits publication of obscene materials in electronic form. Punishment includes up to 3 years imprisonment and ₹10,00,000 fine for first offense, 5 years and ₹10,00,000 for repeat offense.

Section 67A - Sexually Explicit Material:

Criminalizes publication, distribution, and transmission of material containing sexually explicit act. Punishment: 3 years imprisonment and ₹5,00,000 fine for first offense; 5 years and ₹10,00,000 for repeat offense.

Section 67B - Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM):

Criminalizes possession, production, distribution, transmission, and browsing of child sexual exploitation material. Punishment: 5 years imprisonment and ₹10,00,000 fine for first offense; 7 years and ₹10,00,000 for repeat offense. We actively report such material to authorities.

Section 72 - Data Protection:

Mandates confidentiality of information obtained during electronic communications. We implement strict data protection protocols and restrict access to authorized personnel only.

Cybercrimes Prevention Act Amendments:

Recent amendments have strengthened penalties for:

  • Identity theft and impersonation online
  • Phishing and financial fraud
  • Data breach and ransomware attacks
  • Online harassment and cyberbullying

2. CERT-IN Guidelines

The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-IN) provides guidelines for cybersecurity practices. We comply with:

  • Incident Reporting: Mandatory 6-hour reporting timeline for critical security incidents
  • Data Protection: Implementing confidentiality, integrity, and availability measures
  • Vulnerability Management: Regular security assessments and patching
  • Access Control: Multi-factor authentication and least privilege principles
  • Audit Trails: Comprehensive logging of security events
  • Incident Response: 24/7 monitoring and response capabilities

3. National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) Standards

Cyberztalk facilitates crime reporting in compliance with NCRB standards:

  • Proper documentation of crime details
  • Chain of custody for digital evidence
  • Cooperation with law enforcement investigations
  • Adherence to FIR registration procedures
  • Maintenance of audit trails for legal proceedings

4. Data Protection & Privacy Standards

Personal Data Protection

We implement measures aligned with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023:

  • Consent-based data collection
  • Purpose limitation in data usage
  • Storage limitation to necessary period
  • Security safeguards for data protection
  • User rights to access and correction
  • Grievance redressal mechanism

GDPR Compliance (International)

For users from European Union, we comply with General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to be forgotten (data deletion)
  • Data portability provisions
  • Explicit consent management
  • Privacy by design principles
  • Data Protection Impact Assessment

CCPA Compliance (California)

For California users, we provide:

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to delete collected information
  • Right to opt-out of data sales
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising rights

5. Cybersecurity Standards

ISO/IEC 27001 - Information Security Management

We implement controls aligned with this international standard for information security:

  • Information security policies and objectives
  • Access control and user authentication
  • Cryptography and encryption standards
  • Incident management procedures
  • Business continuity planning
  • Regular security assessments

NIST Cybersecurity Framework

We follow NIST guidelines for identifying, protecting, detecting, responding to, and recovering from cyber incidents.

OWASP Security Guidelines

We implement web application security practices to prevent Top 10 vulnerabilities including injection attacks, authentication weaknesses, and sensitive data exposure.
