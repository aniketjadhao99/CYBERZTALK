Cyber Awareness & Digital Hygiene

Learn essential cybersecurity practices for you and your loved ones. Protect yourself, your family, and your organization from digital threats.

Cyber Awareness & Hygiene for Parents

📱 Talk to Your Children About Online Safety

Educate your children about potential online threats such as grooming, bullying, and stalking. Keep track of their online activities and set clear guidelines for internet and online games usage.

⚠️ Notice Indicators of Change in Behavior

If your child begins to spend more time online and starts being defensive or secretive about their online activities, it may be an indicator of cyber grooming. Talk to your child and engage them in other activities.

🛡️ Protect Your Child from Cyber Grooming

Grooming is a practice where someone builds an emotional bond with a child through social media or chat window with an objective of gaining their trust for sexual exploitation.

  • Children may remove privacy settings on social media to make more friends
  • Parents should discuss responsible use of social media
  • Help them select strong privacy settings

⛔ Never Click Suspicious Links or Attachments

Never click on links or files received in e-mail, text message or social media from unknown persons. This may be an attempt to infect computer with malware.

🎥 Cover Your Webcams

A web camera (default in laptops) if hacked/compromised can be leveraged as a medium to observe/watch and record day to day activities. It is recommended to cover webcam when not in use.

🔐 Install Anti-virus Software with Parental Control

Install anti-virus software with parental control functionality or parental control software on the devices used by children and review privacy settings of social media sites used by them.

🔄 Keep Software Updated

Keep your software and Operating system up-to-date. Hackers target software vulnerabilities to access private information and putting you at risk, so make sure to update all your software with the latest security patches.

⚠️ Never install software, games, music and apps from untrusted sources.

🌐 Set Secure Browser Settings

Always choose updated version of the browser and install safe browsing tools for protection yourself from hackers and malware.

Cyber Awareness & Hygiene for Teens & Young Adults

🔒 Secure Your Online Presence

If you have not selected the right settings on your social media accounts, then photos and videos posted can be viewed, downloaded and used by others without your knowledge.

  • Select the right privacy settings and content sharing filters
  • Be selective about accepting friend requests from strangers
  • Learn how to block someone who is making you uncomfortable
  • Learn how to remove someone from your friends list
  • Remember to logout from social media websites after use
  • Secure your phone with a password

📹 Be Mindful of Your Appearance on Video Chat

Your video chats on social media sites can be recorded by the person on the other side. There have been instances where video chats were recorded and shared on social media groups and websites.

Be careful while accepting chat requests from strangers.

📱 Do Not Use Smartphone for Sensitive Photos/Videos

Most smartphones are connected to internet and cloud storage. If a picture or video has been clicked using smartphone connected with the cloud, it may get saved automatically.

Even if you delete photos or videos from your phone, they can be recovered from the cloud account or any other device connected to the cloud using the same account.

🚫 Protect Yourself from Cyber Stalking

Cyber stalkers show advances on a person repeatedly despite clear indication of disinterest. They use internet, email, social media or electronic communication for stalking.

  • Disable location services for social media sites and mobile devices
  • Refrain from sharing personal information with unknown persons
  • Consult relatives and friends if you think you are a victim

⚠️ Beware of Fake Social Media Accounts

Not all accounts are real and not all information provided on accounts are true. Be cautious while accepting friend requests from strangers. If you discover a fake account created in your name, inform the social media service provider immediately so it can be blocked.

💰 Be Cautious with Sensitive Browsing

Browse shopping or banking websites only on a device that belongs to you or on a trusted network. Avoid using friend's phone, public computer, cyber cafe or free Wi-Fi for sensitive browsing as data can be stolen or copied.

⚙️ Be Careful with Device Servicing/Selling

Personal computers and mobile devices contain private information which needs to be erased before sending it for repairing, servicing or selling.

💡 Remember: Deleted data on your communication devices can be recovered!

🔐 Protect Your Communication Devices

Prevent others from accessing your devices by providing password, PIN, Pattern or biometric information. Always install applications from trusted sources only such as Play Store, App Store or official company websites.

🚨 Report Child Sexual Exploitative Material

Any content related to Child Sexual Exploitative and Abuse Material (CSEAM) should be reported to the concerned social media website or the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (www.cybercrime.gov.in)

If anybody shares such content with you, inform them that publication, collection and distribution of CSEAM is illegal.

Cyber Awareness & Hygiene for Organizations

📋 How to Deal with CSEAM in Workplace

All organisations should have clear and strong HR policies on handling content related to Child Sexual Exploitative and Abuse Material (CSEAM).

  • Establish clear rules for use of electronic devices provided by the organisation
  • If any employee is found possessing obscene or indecent content, conduct proper investigation and take action
  • Report any incidence of sharing and storage of obscene content to the police
  • Save a copy of the content as evidence with restricted access
  • Delete all other copies of the content

⚖️ Legal Framework - CSEAM is Illegal

Under the Information Technology Act, 2000:

  • Section 67: Publication and distribution of any material containing sexually explicit act in electronic form is punishable
  • Section 67A: Distribution of sexually explicit content is a punishable offense
  • Section 67B: Criminalizes browsing, downloading, creation, publication and distribution of child pornography

🏢 Organizational Security Practices

Implement comprehensive cyber security measures across the organization:

  • Develop clear IT security policies and procedures
  • Conduct regular security awareness training for all employees
  • Implement multi-factor authentication for sensitive systems
  • Maintain regular data backups and disaster recovery plans
  • Conduct periodic security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Monitor network traffic for suspicious activity

📝 Reporting Mechanisms

Organizations can report cyber crimes and CSEAM incidents through the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (www.cybercrime.gov.in)

Maintain proper documentation of all incidents for legal and compliance purposes.

🔒 Best Practices Summary

Be safe, browse safe! Implement a zero-trust security model, maintain comprehensive logging, conduct regular incident response drills, and ensure all employees understand their role in maintaining organizational cybersecurity.
